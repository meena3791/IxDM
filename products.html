CPS EAGER: Intelligent Agent Incident Command System (ICS) Augmentation

This project will develop a model-based system to support ICS commanders to minimize cognitive load on decision makers, exploit semantic relationships in reports and sensor data to advise of otherwise invisible occurrences, and sequence the actions of ground-level assets to refine causal relationship models to better reflect ongoing developments during crisis and/or event management. The agent-based simulation architecture will demonstrate of the effectiveness of information presentation and transparency in situations where agents can support and enhance human decision-making without increasing the cognitive workload of the human

Android Application for Tinnitus Management

Tinnitus is regarded as the phantom awareness of sound which includes a variety of perceived noises, but not limited to: ringing, buzzing, and in some cases hissing. Ongoing studies using functional neuroimaging also help us identify the regions in the brain that might be responsible for Tinnitus. Although there is no current cure for Tinnitus, there are multiple self-management techniques to help reduce the effects of the disorder. Downloadable applications on smart devices have become a popular tool in managing the effects of Tinnitus. In some cases, these applications incorporated with mindfulness techniques and sound therapy, have become an absolute necessity, and have improved the quality of life of individuals affected by Tinnitus. The purpose of this research is to develop an Android-based application specifically for Tinnitus patients. This application aims to use mindfulness techniques to provide self-coping strategies to Tinnitus patients and measure the effectiveness of those strategies.
